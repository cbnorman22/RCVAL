Ranked Choice Voting in Alabama

What is Ranked Choice Voting?

Ranked Choice Voting (RCV) is a method of voting in which a voter ranks the candidates in order of preference. When the votes are tallied, if no candidate received a majority of the votes (more than 50%), then the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated. Any ballot cast for the eliminated candidate is then cast as a vote for the next ranked choice. The process continues until a candidate has a majority of the votes. In this way, RCV essentially picks out a series of losers until a victor emerges. Studies have found that RCV raises support for elected officials, diversity of candidates, and votes for third party candidates.

Click Here for more information about Ranked Choice Voting.

Where is RCV in Alabama?

Unfortunately, as of October, 2024, the use of Ranked Choice Voting as a voting method is illegal in Alabama under Sections 11-46-10 and 17-1-6 of the Code of Alabama 1975. Subsection A of 17-1-6 reads as follows:

"Ranked-choice voting shall not be used in determining the election or nomination of any candidate to any local, state, or federal office."

This ammendment has cut off the most well proven route to RCV adoption: local government. Typically, it would be easier, more immediately impactful, and practical to support RCV in your local community rather than attempt to jump to statewide adoption. I believe this is still true, but the difficult truth is that pushing for RCV's implementation at all is currently off the table in Alabama. First, this state-level ban must be repealed.

How to Reverse the RCV Ban

There is some good news: The same method used to ban RCV can be used to unban it as well. The state legislature can repeal the ban just as easily as they passed it, but they will not do so without their constituents speaking up. On this page, I have included resources for finding your state senator and state representative. I sincerely wish I could link you to an organized movement in Alabama, but I'm afraid I can't. Better Ballot Alabama, the Alabama branch of a movement for the adoption of RCV has been inactive since the ban was passed in 2023. My hope is that a new organized effort to unban RCV will form over the coming years (It could be you who starts it!), but as it stands, all I would be able to offer is a deadlink to Better Ballot Alabama's website and a link to an inactive Facebook page. It can be disheartening, but I prefer to look at it as an opportunity. Each and every one of you has the potential to kickstart that new effort. When action is difficult, those who act are strong. I encourage you to reach out to your state legislators on the matter. Reach out to friends, family, and colleagues. Organized action is often the result of impactful unorganized actions.

There is another avenue available, however. If the idea of spearheading a new movement feels a bit... much, consider joining a nationwide movement. As I type this, the US Constitution is still the Supreme Law of the Land, and as such, federal laws overrule state laws. If RCV is protected federally (or maybe even adopted for federal elections), that will open the gates once more to community-oriented efforts in Alabama. I have included links to a few nationwide organizations which support the adoption of RCV. Let this also bolster the main message of this page: you are not alone in this. If you care about making our democracy better, if you care about accurately reflecting the will of the people and fostering a healthy spectrum of political ideas:
You are NOT alone

Why Push for RCV instead of *INSERT ISSUE HERE*?

The short answer is that this is the wrong question. Alabama is not short on problems, be they social, political, economic, or a mix of the three. RCV is not a replacement for solving those issues, nor is it intended to be. What RCV provides is a better way to elect people you believe can solve them. Democracy is a tool. It must be wielded responsibly and efficiently, and sometimes it needs maintenance. Support for RCV is untethered from any specific issue of policy or party. The ban on RCV was voted for along party lines in Alabama, but that seems to me to be more about which party is currently in power than any deeper partisan idealogy (The Republican controlled Alaska government adopted RCV statewide). Republicans, Democrats, and Independants all benefit from a well-maintained democracy. RCV fosters solutions to issues, but we the people do the solving. It encourages the change that the public already wishes to see. If there is another, more specific issue that feels more pressing or personal to you, I wholeheartedly urge you to take the resources below and use them to those ends instead. Or support both! There will never be a shortage of issues to care about. Find yours and fight for it. Not tomorrow; TODAY.
